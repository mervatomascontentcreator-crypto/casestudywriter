Case Study: Sample

From Silent in Meetings to Chief Technology Officer

A demonstration piece written to show client-facing case study structure for a coaching/service brand, built around the "before → decision → work → result" framework used in high-converting case studies.

“I would explain something clearly in my head, but by the time I said it out loud, people had already started talking over me... It wasn't that my English was wrong. It just didn't sound confident enough for people to trust it.” Four months of targeted coaching later, he was promoted into the role he'd been passed over for the year prior.

DA-PhilRice Feature

Rising as one above challenges

How a Bicol farmers' association turned pandemic disruption and back-to-back typhoons into their biggest growth year, by supplying relief rice instead of waiting for the disruption to pass.

“We were making about ₱250,000 a year then. After RiceBIS, we started to make millions as we learned to communicate with institutional buyers.” Sales during pandemic relief operations alone brought in ₱3M, and grabbing that opportunity helped push the association's total income from ₱2.3M in 2019 to ₱7.5M in 2020, with assets now worth ₱13.3M.

DA-PhilRice Feature

Women find new aspirations in RiceBIS

Two women who spent decades farming rice for subsistence became rice processors and entrepreneurs, and found something bigger than profit along the way.

“Joining RiceBIS made me realize that I can do more things as an individual for my family and community. When I got married at the age of 18, I thought my life would only revolve around staying at home and taking care of my kids and my husband. But now, I learned that I could achieve more.” Processing palay into brown rice let them sell at ₱50/kg versus ₱16–19/kg through traders.

DA-PhilRice Feature

Deserted farm brings profit to mom agripreneur

A pandemic layoff pushed a massage therapist and her consultant husband to revive her late father's abandoned farm, and they built a second income from it within a season.

A field abandoned for 15 years since her father's passing was, seven months later, teeming with vegetables ready for harvest. “We are happy that we decided to come back because in farming, we found a lot of opportunities to better our lives.” GAP certification alone earned them ₱5 more per kilogram from buyers.
